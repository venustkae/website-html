Good Genes Are Nice, but Joy Is Better

This article delves into the findings of the Harvard Study of Adult Development, one of the world's longest-running studies on adult life.

The research highlights that embracing community and nurturing relationships are key factors in leading a healthy and happy life.

The study followed 268 Harvard sophomores from the 1930s, tracking their physical and mental health over nearly 80 years.

The results underscore the importance of social connections and personal happiness over genetic factors in determining overall well-being.
